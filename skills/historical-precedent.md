# Historical Precedent — Finding and Evaluating Analogies

## When to Use
When you need historical evidence to support or challenge a connection chain.

## Evaluation Rubric
| Criteria | Weight | Weak (1) | Strong (3) |
|----------|--------|----------|------------|
| Mechanism match | 30% | "Vaguely similar event" | "Same causal mechanism" |
| Magnitude match | 20% | "Very different scale" | "Similar severity" |
| Macro context | 25% | "Different rate/inflation regime" | "Similar macro backdrop" |
| Recency | 15% | ">15 years ago" | "<5 years ago" |
| Data quality | 10% | "Anecdotal, no numbers" | "Specific tickers, % moves, timelines" |

**Threshold**: Only use cases scoring ≥ 2.0/3.0.

## Structure for Each Precedent
```
Event: [What happened]
Date: [When]
Mechanism: [Why it matters — the causal chain]
Market reaction: [Ticker/Sector]: [Direction] [Magnitude] over [Timeframe]
Sustained or reverted: [Did the move stick?]
Key lesson: [One sentence takeaway]
Relevance to current event: [Why this parallel matters / what's different]
```

## Integration Rules
- **Score ≥ 2.5**: Lead with this precedent — strong anchor
- **Score 2.0-2.5**: Supporting evidence with caveats
- **Score < 2.0**: Mention briefly or skip

## Anti-Patterns
- ❌ Cherry-picking only supportive cases — always look for cases where the expected reaction DIDN'T happen
- ❌ Single case as proof — one precedent is anecdote, two is pattern, three is evidence
- ❌ Ignoring macro regime — ZIRP precedent may not apply in high-rate environment
- ❌ Treating all moves as permanent — most event-driven moves revert in 2-4 weeks
- ❌ Over-searching — 1-2 targeted searches enough. If nothing good, acknowledge novelty.

## Search Query Patterns
- Specific event + market: "yield curve inversion 2019 sector rotation"
- Quantitative: "NVDA earnings surprise stock price reaction history"
- Time-bounded: Add "2020-2025" for recency
- Always search for COUNTER-EXAMPLES alongside supporting cases
